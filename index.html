Brief History

Roles on Set

Director

Although this role's responsibilities on set will change depending on the project, a director's job is essentially to 'run the show'. They oversee every step of the process, from selecting actors in pre-production, to editing and colouring in post. Some directors are also the writer of the script, albeit this is not always the case.

Cinematograhper

This role's responsibilities pertain to the light and camera set ups for every shot in a film. Cinematographers are responsible for conveying the directors vision throughout the film. They foces on over all aesthetic of the shot by using multiply different tactics, such as: shot list, lighting set-ups, camera setup + movements, etc.

The 'Greats'

Stanley Kubrick

Kubrick is known as one of the most iconic filmmakers of all time. He has a very particular style that captivates the audience the minute the roll begins. He is well known for capturing the 'dark side of human nature', and his iconic symmetric compisition.

Best Known For:

  • 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)
  • The Shining (1980)
  • A Clockwork Orange (1971)
  • Lolita (1962)

Wes Anderson

Majoring in Philosophy in University, Anderson met Owen Wilson (actor). They began making short films which were well recieved, and this snowballed into a high-profile career and a cult audience for the pair. Wes Anderson is well spotted in films as he has a very strict colour pallette for each individual film which is almost exclusively pastel. He is also best spotted by his perfect symmetry is every shot he produces.

Best Known For:

  • The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014)
  • The French Dispatch (2021)
  • Fantastic Mr. Fox (2009)
  • Asteroid City (2023)
